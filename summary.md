# Executive Summary

This analysis investigates the relationship between Bitcoin market sentiment and trader performance using Hyperliquid trading data and the Fear & Greed Index.

After cleaning and merging the datasets at the daily level, several performance and behavioral metrics were analyzed, including average profit and loss (PnL), win rate, trade frequency, trade size, and directional trading behavior.

The results indicate a clear relationship between sentiment and trader outcomes. Average profitability was highest during Greed and Extreme Greed periods, suggesting that optimistic market environments provide more favorable trading conditions. Traders also tended to increase position sizes during these periods, reflecting greater confidence and risk appetite.

Trader segmentation further revealed that frequent traders generated substantially higher cumulative profits than infrequent traders. A clustering analysis identified distinct trader archetypes, highlighting meaningful differences in trading activity and profitability across accounts.

Based on these findings, two practical recommendations are proposed. First, traders may consider increasing participation during Greed-driven market regimes while maintaining disciplined risk controls. Second, during Fear and Extreme Fear periods, traders should adopt a more defensive approach by reducing exposure and focusing on high-conviction opportunities.

Overall, the analysis demonstrates that market sentiment is closely associated with both trader behavior and performance, and that sentiment-aware trading strategies may improve decision-making and risk management.
